Mixed weather in store for Telangana & Hyderabad for next week: IMD

TNN / Updated: Mar 10, 2024, 11:23 IST
Temperature set to rise further
Mixed weather conditions with increasing temperatures and occasional rainfall expected in Telangana and Hyderabad. Heatwave-like conditions may occur in March. Lingampally records a temperature of 37.6°C. Ekm, Delhi, and Mumbai also experience temperature variations.
HYDERABAD: A mixed bag of weather conditions is expected to persist over Telangana and Hyderabad over the next week, with a gradual increase in temperatures alongside occasional light rainfall, according to officials from the India Meteorological Department (IMD).
“A rise in temperatures will continue, with a slight relief anticipated next week owing to sporadic thundershowers.
However, if rainfall remains scarce, cloud cover is expected to provide some relief from the heat,” said A Sravani, scientist ‘C’ at IMD.
She further added, “Towards the end of March, temperatures are projected to rise steadily, potentially leading to heatwave-like conditions across the state.”
Even as there is no heatwave in Telangana, the temperatures remained notably high for last three days. Over the past three days, the highest recorded temperatures in Telangana have surpassed 39° Celsius. The highest temperatures were recorded at 39.7° Celsius in Mancherial, Nirmal, and Mancherial on Saturday.
Despite cloud cover in the city in the evening, mercury levels in Lingampally registered 37.6° Celsius. tnn
